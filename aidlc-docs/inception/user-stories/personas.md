# Personas — HOA Management System

---

## Persona 1: Resident

**Name**: Maria Santos
**Age**: 38
**Occupation**: Working professional, unit owner
**Tech Comfort**: Mixed — uses smartphone daily for social media and banking, but not highly technical

### Goals
- Pay HOA dues quickly without going to the office
- Track the status of maintenance requests she submitted
- Book the clubhouse for family events without calling the admin
- Stay informed about community announcements and events
- Issue visitor passes for guests without paperwork

### Frustrations
- Having to call the property manager for every small request
- Not knowing if her maintenance request was received or acted on
- Missing announcements because they were posted on a physical bulletin board
- Complicated interfaces that require too many steps

### Key Workflows
- Register and manage her digital profile
- View and pay monthly HOA dues via GCash
- Submit and track maintenance requests with photos
- Book amenities and receive confirmation
- Issue digital visitor passes for guests
- View community announcements, vote in polls, RSVP to events
- Download HOA documents (policies, forms)

## Persona 2: HOA Board Member

**Name**: Roberto Dela Cruz
**Age**: 55
**Occupation**: Retired engineer, elected HOA Board President
**Tech Comfort**: Moderate — comfortable with email and web browsing, needs clear navigation

### Goals
- Have real-time visibility into HOA finances and collection rates
- Communicate important decisions and announcements to all residents efficiently
- Review and approve maintenance and amenity requests
- Conduct polls and gather resident feedback digitally
- Ensure the community is compliant with data privacy regulations

### Frustrations
- Manually compiling financial reports from spreadsheets
- Low resident participation in polls and meetings
- Difficulty tracking which announcements residents have seen
- Lack of a central place for all community documents

### Key Workflows
- View financial dashboards (budget vs. expenses, collection rate)
- Post community announcements and send push/SMS alerts
- Create and publish polls and feedback forms
- Create events and track RSVPs
- Upload and manage community documents
- View incident reports and analytics dashboards
- Manage resident engagement metrics

## Persona 3: Property Manager

**Name**: Liza Reyes
**Age**: 42
**Occupation**: Full-time HOA Property Manager
**Tech Comfort**: High — uses management software daily, comfortable with dashboards and workflows

### Goals
- Manage all resident requests and maintenance assignments from one dashboard
- Track billing, overdue accounts, and payment records efficiently
- Assign and monitor maintenance tasks to staff
- Manage amenity bookings and schedules
- Generate reports for board meetings quickly

### Frustrations
- Residents calling or texting her directly for requests that should be self-service
- Manually chasing overdue payments
- No visibility into maintenance staff workload and performance
- Spending hours compiling reports manually

### Key Workflows
- Manage resident directory and profiles
- Generate and manage billing invoices; record manual payments
- Assign maintenance requests to staff; track resolution progress
- Manage amenity availability and approve/reject bookings
- Upload documents to the repository
- Post announcements and manage communication
- View and export analytics and financial reports
- Review incident reports from gate guards and residents

## Persona 4: Gate Guard

**Name**: Jun Bautista
**Age**: 29
**Occupation**: Security guard stationed at the HOA entrance gate
**Tech Comfort**: Basic — comfortable with simple tablet apps, needs large touch targets and clear UI
**Device**: Tablet (touch-optimized, may be used in low-light conditions)

### Goals
- Quickly verify if a visitor has a valid pass without calling the resident
- Confirm resident identity and unit number in seconds
- Log incidents digitally without paper forms
- Have a simple, fast interface that works under pressure

### Frustrations
- Paper-based visitor logs that are slow and error-prone
- Having to call residents to verify every visitor
- No way to quickly look up resident information
- Incident reports getting lost or delayed

### Key Workflows
- Scan or manually look up visitor QR code passes
- Verify resident identity and unit number in real time
- Approve or deny visitor entry based on pass validity
- Submit incident reports with description, photo, date/time, and location
- View today's expected visitors list
